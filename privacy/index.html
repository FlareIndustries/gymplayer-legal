GYMPLAYER PRIVACY POLICY Last updated: 13 March 2026 1. WHO WE ARE GymPlayer is provided by Kobi Morris ("we", "us", "our"). Contact details: - Privacy contact: GymPlayerSupport@gmail.com This Privacy Policy explains how GymPlayer handles information when you use the app and related support channels. 2. HOW GYMPLAYER WORKS GymPlayer is designed primarily as a local-first iOS app. In the current version we did not identify an in-app account system, a general-purpose cloud sync service operated by us, or third-party advertising and cross-app tracking SDKs. Most library data stays on your device unless: - you ask the app to look up metadata or artwork from third-party music databases, - you use Apple in-app purchase features, - you join or host a nearby-device Tether session, - you export or share content using Apple's system share tools, or - you contact us for support. 3. INFORMATION WE HANDLE We may handle the following categories of information. A. Audio files and library content you choose to import If you import audio files, GymPlayer copies the selected files into the app's local storage so they can be managed and played inside the app. GymPlayer is not intended to modify the original source file you selected in Files or another external location. We also store related library information locally on your device, such as: - song titles, artists, file references and imported library entries, - playlists, favourites and queue information, - custom song artwork and playlist artwork, - metadata edits you make, - playback settings and app preferences, and - auto-import folder bookmarks and related import-tracking data. B. Photos and images you choose If you choose artwork from your photo library, GymPlayer reads the selected image and stores the artwork you apply in the app's local library data. We do not automatically upload that artwork to our servers. C. Camera recordings, edited videos and exports If you use GymPlayer's pose recording or video editing features, video files and exports are created locally on your device. If you share an export, that transfer happens only when you choose to use Apple's share sheet and select a destination or service. D. Nearby-device Tether session data If you use Tether, GymPlayer exchanges limited session data with nearby devices you connect to. Depending on the session, this may include: - your device name as reported by iOS, - the Tether session code, - playback state and timing information, - track descriptors such as title, artist, filename stem, duration, file size and matching fingerprint data, and - temporary audio asset files required for the session. GymPlayer uses Apple's Multipeer Connectivity framework with required session encryption for Tether sessions. Temporary Tether session files are intended to be cached locally and removed when the session stops or a participating peer disconnects, although immediate deletion cannot be guaranteed in every interrupted or abnormal shutdown scenario. E. Metadata rescue and artwork lookup requests If you use metadata rescue or related artwork lookup features, GymPlayer may send search terms derived from the track or file you selected, such as song title, artist, or filename-based queries, to third-party music data providers. In the current version those providers include: - Apple iTunes Search, - Deezer, and - MusicBrainz. If matching artwork is available, GymPlayer may also download artwork images from third-party URLs and save the selected artwork locally on your device. F. Subscription and App Store purchase information If you buy, restore or use GymPlayer Pro, product availability, pricing, billing and payment processing are handled by Apple through the App Store and Apple's in-app purchase systems. GymPlayer stores local entitlement state on your device so subscription features can be unlocked, but we do not receive your full payment card number or other full payment instrument details from Apple. G. Support and communications If you contact us, we may handle the information you provide in that request, such as your name, email address, device details, screenshots, and the content of your message. 4. INFORMATION WE DO NOT CURRENTLY INTEND TO COLLECT DIRECTLY Based on the current codebase review for this draft, GymPlayer is not intended to operate its own user account system or its own remote content-hosting platform. We also did not identify a third-party advertising SDK, cross-app tracking SDK, or general analytics SDK embedded in the app at the time of drafting this policy. That statement applies to the current version reviewed for this draft. If new SDKs, accounts, cloud features, diagnostics, advertising or tracking tools are added later, this policy and your App Store privacy disclosures should be updated before release. 5. HOW WE USE INFORMATION We use handled information to: - import, organise, play and manage your audio library, - store your playlists, favourites, artwork and settings, - support auto-import folder access that you explicitly configure, - provide Tether session discovery, synchronisation and temporary peer-to-peer transfers, - perform metadata rescue and artwork matching at your request, - enable camera recording, video editing, export and sharing features you choose to use, - confirm and restore GymPlayer Pro entitlements through Apple, - provide customer support, troubleshoot issues and maintain app integrity, and - comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms. 6. WHEN INFORMATION IS SHARED We do not sell your personal information. We may share or disclose limited information in the following situations: - Apple. When you use the App Store, in-app purchases, restore purchases or related Apple services, Apple handles information under its own terms and privacy practices. - Metadata providers. If you use metadata rescue or artwork lookup, your search terms and related request data are sent to the third-party provider you are querying, currently Apple iTunes Search, Deezer and MusicBrainz. - Nearby devices you choose to connect. If you use Tether, session information and temporary shared assets are sent directly to the participating nearby device or devices in that session. - Share destinations you choose. If you export or share media using the iOS share sheet, the selected app, service or recipient will receive the content you choose to share and will handle it under its own terms and privacy policy. - Service providers or advisors. We may disclose information where reasonably necessary to obtain hosting, support, legal or compliance assistance in connection with operating GymPlayer or responding to an issue. - Legal requirements. We may disclose information if required by law, court order, regulatory request or to protect rights, safety or property. 7. INTERNATIONAL DISCLOSURE GymPlayer is developed for users who may be located in Australia and elsewhere. If you use Apple services, metadata rescue, artwork lookup, support tools or other third-party services, limited information may be disclosed to recipients outside Australia, including recipients operating in the United States and other countries where those providers or their infrastructure operate. Those third parties operate under their own privacy practices and may process information overseas. We do not control the independent privacy terms of Apple, Deezer, MusicBrainz or any destination you choose through the share sheet. 8. STORAGE, RETENTION AND DELETION GymPlayer stores most app data locally on your device, including imported files, artwork, playlists, library metadata and settings. Retention generally works as follows: - imported songs and local library data remain until you delete them in the app or remove the app from your device, - selected artwork and metadata edits remain until you replace or delete them, - auto-import bookmarks and related settings remain until you remove or reset them, - Tether session cache files are intended to be removed when the session ends or peers disconnect, and - support communications may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to respond, resolve the issue, keep records, or comply with legal obligations. Because the app is largely local-first, you can often manage deletion directly by deleting content in the app, removing exported files from your device or chosen destination, revoking permissions in iOS Settings, or uninstalling GymPlayer. 9. SECURITY We use reasonable technical measures appropriate to the app's design, including reliance on iOS app sandboxing, Apple's platform permission controls, security-scoped access to user-selected files and folders where applicable, and required encryption for Tether sessions through Apple's Multipeer Connectivity session configuration. No system is completely secure. You are responsible for securing access to your device, reviewing the apps and destinations you choose to share content with, and using lawful, trusted sources when importing media. 10. YOUR CHOICES AND RIGHTS You can control many privacy-related actions directly inside GymPlayer or through iOS settings. For example, you can: - choose whether to grant camera, photo library and local network permissions, - delete imported media, playlists, artwork and other local content, - remove or change configured auto-import folders, - stop using Tether, metadata rescue or export/sharing features, and - cancel a GymPlayer Pro subscription in your Apple account settings. Depending on the law that applies to you, you may also have rights to request access to, correction of, or information about personal information we hold off-device, such as support correspondence. To make a request, contact us using the details in section 1. Because GymPlayer does not currently use an in-app account system in the reviewed version, there is no general account deletion workflow. Most app data is stored on your device and can be removed locally. 11. CHILDREN GymPlayer is not directed to children in a manner intended to collect personal information from them directly. If you believe a child has provided us off-device personal information, such as through a support request, contact us so we can review and address it. 12.
